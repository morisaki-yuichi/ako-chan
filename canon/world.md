# World & Setting

Geography, locations, rules, and seasonal facts. Update as stories establish new details.

## Core Premise

A globe-spanning adventure set in the **modern present-day world**.
Fantasy and science-fiction elements are woven in, but Earth's biology,
geography, ecosystems, and animal behavior are depicted as faithfully as
possible. The one constant deviation: animals can speak to each other — and
Ako-chan alone can understand them.

## Key Locations

### England — Home Base
- Ako-chan and Dango's starting point and final destination
- Temperate, green; the familiar comfort that the rest of the world is measured against
- Details to be filled in as the opening stories establish them

### Antarctica — The Finale
- The coldest, most hostile environment the group reaches
- Home of Pen-san and Colonel Adelie
- Site of Yu-chan's hidden base and the root cause of the global anomalies
- Obia's inventions are especially critical here — no unprotected mammal or reptile could survive unaided

**Antarctica — arrival and inland (11-01 onwards)**
- Coast: flat ice shelf with clean round breathing holes (Weddell seal territory); dark ocean between ice; no birds at arrival; sound carries differently — footsteps echo back changed from ice walls
- The ice continent viewed from the boat: not a mountain, not a cliff — "like the world has stopped and decided to be white" (Ako-chan's diary); ice colors include blue, white, green
- Wind: stops without warning; when still, silence is total and feels ancient; wind direction shifts unpredictably, especially near the ice ridge
- Temperature on arrival: −12°C; drops further inland
- Low-frequency signal from the structure: detectable by Daru's shell and Gabu's directional sense; regular beat, intentionally transmitted; gets stronger as the group moves inland
- **Ice valley** (11-12 onwards): accessed by crossing a ridge south of the landing site; walls go straight down in blue/white/green; floor flat and pale; vast scale; sound of the structure is louder here; Gabu: "Correct — exactly what it should be"
- **The structure** (detected, not yet entered as of 11-30): 12km south of the landing site; inland; below ice; large enough for multiple rooms; running on significant power; access via one vertical shaft (~1m wide) reaching the surface; access point at 210° magnetic from the ice valley; electromagnetic interference affects instruments near the ice ridge
  - Has been active for at least 40 years; ice formation confirms long occupation
  - Emits blue light detectable from below ice (Weddell seal testimony)
  - The surrounding ice is denser and smoother than normal — no air pockets (Colonel Adelie's observation)
  - Generates an artificial electromagnetic field and regular low-frequency transmissions
  - Pen-san heard its sound from inside the egg; first sound after hatching
- **Yu-chan's base** (entered 12-02): accessed through a vertical shaft at 210° magnetic; the gate opened automatically for Obia — no touch, no command; inside air is warm and steady
  - Main corridor leads to a large round room with wall-sized screens showing all recorded locations from the journey
  - Central room: giant monitor; Yu-chan communicates from here; always with back turned; face never shown
  - 21 anomalies controlled from this base; all shut down remotely by Yu-chan in 12-16
  - The base recognized Obia specifically; designed to open for Obia's arrival

### Other Locations

**France / Paris** (02-01 – 02-07)
- Crossed via Eurostar through Channel Tunnel (02-01)
- Paris: overwhelming sensory city — bread, coffee, petrol, old stone, flowers; Dango needed extra time to process the smells
- Eiffel Tower visited; Seine river walked; outdoor markets with many cats
- Green fields, grey February sky; noticeably different light from England

**Belgium** (02-07 – 02-09)
- Flat brown farmland extending to the horizon; train from Paris to Brussels through green-brown countryside
- Large field near a village where Dango ran free (02-08)
- Small farm villages; Belgian sheepdogs; heavy February fog events

**Netherlands / Amsterdam** (02-10 – 02-11)
- City of canals; old houses lean toward the water; dark still water in winter
- Canal paths; old stone bridges; moored boats along edges
- Duck community lives on the canals

**Germany** (02-12 – 02-19)
- Entry from Belgium/Netherlands; markets with sausage grills
- Southern German forests: old, wide-trunked trees; narrow leaf-and-snow paths; very quiet in winter
- Rhine river: wide, grey-green, fast-moving; old castle towers on the far bank
- Rain in February; construction sites in cities
- Small guesthouses and cafés

**Austria** (02-21 – 02-25)
- Alpine environment: mountains very large with snow-capped peaks; dark green lower slopes
- Mountain air: pine, cold rock, thin and clean — triggers strong instinctive response in Dango
- Mountain paths with snow; marmots hibernate (Oct–Apr) in the rocks
- Mountain refuges available as emergency shelters (with blankets, supplies, small stove)
- Blizzards can arrive without warning; visibility can drop to near zero in under a minute
- Small mountain villages with wooden inns and big fires

**Balkans (Slovenia / border region)** (02-26 onwards)
- Roads become smaller; signs shift from German to Slovenian and then unknown languages
- Border checkpoints exist; smaller roads often faster than official routes
- Small villages with chickens; crows watch from bare winter trees
- Guesthouses with west-facing windows; hills in the direction of home (England)

**Austria (Vienna)** (03-01)
- Vienna is an old, grand city with wide streets and tall white carved buildings
- Old quarter traversable on foot

**Hungary / Budapest** (03-02 – 03-03)
- Budapest has bridges over the wide Danube and hills with old buildings
- Colourful markets with fruit, scarves, roasted nuts; main squares with street performers
- Budapest Zoo: old zoo with old trees; notable elder tortoise resident

**Serbia / Belgrade** (03-04 – 03-13)
- Road through hills covered in low trees and brown grass; wide sky; distant village with white church
- Belgrade has a long riverside park; good for an off-lead run; local dogs present in the park
- Small roadside cafés with simple soup and bread; warm interiors
- Mountain animals (wolves, deer, foxes) descending from Serbian hills in numbers — something wrong above

**Bosnia** (03-14 – 03-15)
- War memorials in some towns — walls of photographs with names and dates
- Hill clearings suitable for camping; very clear night skies in the hills

**Dalmatian Coast (Croatia / Montenegro region)** (03-16 – 03-29)
- Adriatic Sea: distinctly deep, clear, still blue — different from Channel or North Sea
- Stone fishing villages: pale old stone, worn smooth; narrow streets to small harbours; old red-tile rooftops
- Many cats living on walls and windowsills
- Sea floor: faint fixed blue glow visible from dock on clear nights; fish avoid the area above it
- Offshore: fish rise and sink in a perfectly regular nightly cycle — not natural behaviour
- Small uninhabited island accessible by short boat trip; wild cat colony (15+) led by a grey elder
- Seawater: anomalous electrical signal detected by Obia, weak but measurable; larger scale than Amsterdam
- Coast road south: vegetation decreases, rocks increase as you go further south toward Greece
- Natural stone arch: sea-carved arch framing the open Adriatic; a landmark of the coast

**Albania** (03-30 – 04-12)
- Border post on a mountain road; narrow older asphalt; mountains close and large
- Different tree species and ground plants from the Balkans further north
- Mediterranean limestone geology; air warmer and sharper
- Sea visible to the south between mountain passes
- Rocky sunny slopes in the south hold warmth for a long time after sunset — good thermal mass for ectotherms
- Daru (Dalmatian Hermann's Tortoise) found on a warm rocky slope in the southern part of the country

**Greece** (04-13 onwards)
- Crossed from Albania; border quiet; road looks the same on both sides but the soil and air change immediately
- Limestone geology; low silver-green scrubland; abundant olive groves with old twisted trunks
- Light distinctly sharper and clearer than the Balkans — Mediterranean quality
- Daru noticed the soil difference immediately: "older, like the ground has been in the sun for a very long time"
- Olive groves: thick fallen-leaf litter under old trees; good habitat for small tortoises
- Villages: stone walls, fig trees in the square, elder cats who observe travellers carefully
- Goats graze at the edges of the olive groves; good sources of local information
- Gabu (Arabian Greek Tortoise) found in olive-leaf litter under an old tree in northern Greece

**Turkey / Istanbul** (05-01 – 05-03)
- Crossed from northern Greece through Thrace into European Istanbul
- Istanbul: enormous city of 12+ million people; overwhelming for first arrival
- Grand Bazaar: thousands of shops, thick air of spices, leather, perfume — Dango overwhelmed by sensory overload
- Bosphorus: wide dark-blue strait separating Europe and Asia; cats observe it constantly; orange cat reported south-light pillar
- Anomaly: pillar of light seen from south, twice — consistent with 02-03 Paris sparrow report

**Turkey / Central Anatolia** (05-04 – 05-08)
- Inland from Istanbul: air gets warmer, ground drier, trees shorter as the road moves east
- Cappadocia: fairy chimneys — tall pillar rocks, smooth and difficult to climb; pale dry land; wide blue sky
- Kangal country (Central Anatolia): Kangal livestock guardian dogs, very large sand-coloured dogs; guard sheep in the hills
- Heat rises above 30°C by midday; both tortoise shelters required cooling function upgrade

**Turkey / Eastern Anatolia → Armenian border** (05-09)
- Road becomes emptier as the group moves east; anomaly density highest near Armenia border
- Sudden storm near Armenian border: pressure dropped too fast, unusual rotation pattern; Obia: "may not be natural"

**Armenia / Lake Sevan** (05-10 – 05-13)
- High altitude country: roads climb, air thin and cool, green hills
- Lake Sevan: high-altitude lake (approx. 1,900m); snowmelt-cold (~7°C); clear mirror-like surface reflects sky perfectly
- Stunned the whole group into silence; even Daru stopped mid-sentence
- Old tortoises live near the south shore under stone walls — one told Gabu "you came from far away; that alone is something great"
- Anomaly: Obia received second signal here at night (same frequency as 02-15, stronger, from south)

**Georgia** (05-14 – 05-17)
- Mountain pass from Armenia into Georgia: narrow road, wet stone, steep drops into deep valleys
- Tbilisi old quarter: very narrow winding alleys, carved wooden balconies overhanging the lanes, painted facades
- Small courtyard with fountain inside the old quarter (Dango found here after getting separated)
- Georgian mountain ridge: wolf bowed its head to the group without words — unexplained
- Georgian village: child built pebble bridge for a beetle — moved Gabu

**China / Silk Road — Xinjiang to Shanghai** (06-01 – 06-07)
- Silk Road entry from Kazakhstan: flat dry land, pale sky, enormous horizon, warm ground
- Near Great Wall (northern China): long grey stone line along hilltops; dry yellow grass; red foxes present
- Shanghai: enormous city; Huangpu River wide and busy; morning quiet on the lower stone steps of the river embankment; small river otters present
- Shanghai at night: skyline of towers with lights visible as "stars that fell down" (Daru); river reflections; warm heavy air

**Japan / Hakata (Fukuoka)** (06-10 – 06-11)
- Arrived by ferry from China; Hakata Port busy with carts, trucks, seabirds on rooftops
- Cats communicate in Japanese but Ako-chan understands perfectly — her ability is confirmed language-independent
- Word of "the one who listens" has traveled ahead through cat networks across Asia
- Hakata's large-billed crows are confident, organized local guides; the crow checked all yatai and recommends one

**Japan / Shinkansen corridor** (06-12)
- Shinkansen very fast; landscape of green hills, tunnels, brief sea views, small towns
- Rice paddies: flooded fields with neat rows of small green plants — green in June, gold in autumn
- Gabu's discovery: "Things grow in water?" — rice paddies are a new concept for a land-born tortoise

**Japan / Tokyo** (06-13 – 06-14)
- Very large and very loud; Gabu retreats into shelter within minutes
- Many small dogs being carried in Tokyo; Dango requests and receives the same treatment (collar deep gold)
- Alley cats in Tokyo have detailed information about animals across Japan — networks well-established

**Ecuador coast** (09-01)
- Pacific coast of South America; tropical green hills; warm water
- Frigate birds and blue-footed boobies present in large numbers
- Seabird networks carry information across the Pacific — they knew the group was coming

**Galapagos Islands** (09-02 – 09-04)
- Volcanic island group; dark basaltic rock; warm dry air
- Native wildlife (iguanas, birds, giant tortoises) shows no fear of humans
- Galapagos giant tortoise: shell size of small car; one step covers the distance of a full tortoise sprint; individuals can exceed 120 years
- Oldtimer animal that can speak of time on a scale no mammal can match

**Peru coast / Humboldt Current** (09-05 – 09-06)
- Cold ocean current runs along the coast of Peru even near the equator; water 8–12°C in this region
- South American fur seals (*Arctocephalus australis*) colonize rocky coastal outcrops in large numbers
- Sea otters can swim without temperature-regulation wetsuit in Humboldt Current waters

**Peru / Machu Picchu** (09-07)
- High-altitude Inca city built on a mountain peak; accessed by climbing into the mountains from the coast
- Stone construction without mortar; terraces and temples; surrounded by cloud-filled valleys
- No explanation adds to the experience of the first view — Ako-chan confirmed this

**Chile / Atacama Desert** (09-10 – 09-12)
- Driest place on Earth; salt flats and pale stone; almost no vegetation; no animals in the open
- Air so dry that Riro's hydration unit required emergency supplemental device
- Andean fox (*Lycalopex culpaeus*) inhabits the edges; can detect low-frequency signals from the south without any instrument
- Local guanacos and flamingos have shifted ranges unusually northward

**Chile / Santiago** (09-15)
- Large modern city; glass and stone; parks with fenced trees; wide roads
- No forest environment; Suke-san noted absence of connected tree-cover immediately

**Chile / Lake District** (09-16)
- Southern Chile: dense temperate forest and deep cold lakes; trees go to every horizon
- Climate and forest structure comparable to Hokkaido; Suke-san immediately recognized it as "correct"

**Patagonia / Torres del Paine** (09-17 – 09-21)
- High ridge paths with enormous sky; strong constant wind
- Three granite towers (*Las Torres*) rise almost vertically; visible from far distances
- Glacial lakes: water 3°C; pale blue-white color; ice fragments floating; connected to active glaciers
- Puma (*Puma concolor*) territory; individuals may observe travelers from ridgelines without hunting intent

**Tierra del Fuego** (10-01 – 10-03)
- Southernmost inhabited land; sky is distinctly lower, wider, and differently colored than anywhere before
- Strong lateral (sideways) wind; Patagonian fox (*Lycalopex culpaeus magellanicus*) present
- Fox local knowledge: "Those who go past this point south do not arrive by chance — there is always a reason"

**Drake Passage approach / Tierra del Fuego shore** (10-04 – 10-20)
- Dramatic grey sea beyond the coastal hills; waves come in rolling hills from every direction at once
- Nothing on the far horizon — only sea and matching grey sky
- Shore suitable for boat preparation; one sheltered bay for overnight anchoring

**Drake Passage** (10-25 – 10-31)
- Approximately 800 km wide between southern South America and the Antarctic Peninsula
- Considered one of the most dangerous stretches of ocean on Earth
- Waves come from multiple directions simultaneously; no landmass to break the Southern Ocean circulation
- Deep ocean floor transmits artificial signals — detected by Riro and confirmed by Obia; origin point fixed to the south
- Drake midpoint: location equidistant from all continental land — "the farthest place from any land"
- Crossing time: approximately 2–3 days at moderate speed; waves diminish slightly near Antarctic Peninsula

**Chile / Puerto Natales** (09-27 – 09-29)
- Small harbor town at the gateway to the far south; brightly colored rooftops against grey mountain sky
- Local people describe it as the last town before "the real south"
- Small harbor with fishing boats; old garden at a house near the dock

**Japan / Hokkaido** (06-16 – 06-30+)
- Air changes immediately upon crossing into Hokkaido: cool, clean, full of tall wide trees; sky very wide
- Soil is dark volcanic, rich in minerals — different smell from anything since Austria
- Coast has a hill with clear views of the sea; from the top, the current-line where cold meets warm water is visible as a color difference
- Suke-san's territory: the hill, a hidden hollow in the hillside, a large old tree root (dry even in heavy rain), a flat rock facing morning sun
- Rocky bay below the east cliff: cold tidal rocks; the location where Riro (sea otter) is found sitting alone (06-28 onwards)
- Hokkaido foxes have thick fur; they observe the coast carefully and carry local information
- June in Hokkaido: cooler than mainland Japan; no full rainy season; clear nights with many stars

**Caspian Coast / Kazakhstan Steppe** (05-18 onwards)
- After Georgia: land flattens rapidly near the Caspian coast; low grass, hard ground, straight wide paths
- Kazakhstan steppe: grass to every horizon; trees and hills both disappear; sky enormous and all-around
- Daytime: hawks circle very high; golden eagles observed group for over an hour (05-23)
- Night: no lights anywhere; stars so dense the sky looks white; Milky Way spans horizon to horizon
- Nomad horses graze the steppe; one reported the south wind has "changed smell" since last year
- Silk Road caravan routes still used by camels; caravan reported mass southward animal movement from the east
- Chinese border mountains visible on eastern horizon by end of May (05-30)

## Rules & Lore

### Animal Speech
- All animals in this world can speak to one another
- **Only Ako-chan can understand animal speech.** All other humans hear only normal animal sounds
- Animals behave otherwise exactly as they would in the real world — instincts, habitat needs, diets, and physical limits all apply

### Biological Realism
- Animals cannot survive outside their natural habitat without assistance
- A sea otter cannot travel inland; a tortoise cannot tolerate polar cold; an emperor penguin chick cannot leave Antarctica unaided
- **Obia exists specifically to solve this.** Each new companion joins the group only after Obia has built or adapted a device that makes travel possible for that species

### Obia's Inventions
- Obia identifies each companion's biological constraints before they become a crisis
- Inventions are grounded in plausible (if advanced) science — not magic
- A log of inventions should be added here as the stories establish them

### Yu-chan's Test
- Unexplained disturbances and crises occur across the globe throughout the journey
- These are not coincidences — they are engineered by Yu-chan as trials
- The companions do not learn this until the Antarctic climax
- Yu-chan monitors everything through Obia; Obia's loyalty to the group is genuine, but the surveillance is real

### Tone
- Stories balance warmth and wonder with genuine stakes
- Each companion's personality and species traits drive how problems are solved
- Growth comes from friction: Gabu's caution vs. Daru's boldness, Suke-san's pride vs. Obia's precision, Pen-san's self-importance vs. the group's needs
- The underlying question — *does humanity deserve to survive?* — is answered through how Ako-chan and her companions treat the world and each other

## Seasons & Climate

*(Add location-specific seasonal notes as stories establish them.)*

- **England (January)**: Cold and grey; bare trees; the world feels quiet and waiting
- **France / Belgium / Netherlands (February)**: Cold and grey; bare trees; Paris smells richly even in winter; Belgian farmland flat and brown; Amsterdam canals dark and still
- **Germany (February)**: Rainy and cold; southern forests very quiet — bird population notably depleted
- **Austria (February)**: Alpine winter; heavy snowfall possible; clear days with sharp cold; mountain air distinctly pine-and-rock; blizzards without warning
- **Balkans (February)**: Smaller roads, colder hills; signs shift language at each border; sparse villages
- **Vienna / Budapest / Serbia (March)**: Cold but beginning to warm; pale thin sun; wide flat skies over the plains; river flooding unusual for the season
- **Dalmatian Coast (March)**: Warm enough for real sun on the face; Mediterranean blue sea; stone villages; active lizards on south-facing walls; cats everywhere
- **Albania (late March – mid April)**: Warmer than Balkans; limestone geography; sharper plant smell; sea visible between mountain passes; rocky slopes hold warmth long after sunset
- **Greece (mid April onwards)**: Mediterranean limestone; olive groves; silver-green scrubland; sharp clear light; noticeably warmer than Albania; soil has a different, older quality
- **Turkey / Istanbul (early May)**: Hot and overwhelming; Grand Bazaar air thick with mixed scents; Bosphorus cool but open; cats everywhere
- **Turkey / Central Anatolia (early–mid May)**: Dry heat, above 30°C by midday; landscape opens; Cappadocia pale and lunar; Kangal dog country in the hills
- **Armenia / Lake Sevan (mid May)**: High altitude cool; lake water cold (~7°C snowmelt); clear thin air; old stone walls and green hills
- **Georgia (mid May)**: Wet mountain passes; Tbilisi warmer in the valley; carved balconies, old stone lanes; Georgian forests dark
- **Kazakhstan steppe (late May)**: Continental; hot by day, cold at night; no trees, no hills; stars extraordinary without any light pollution
- **China / Xinjiang–Silk Road (early June)**: Hot and dry; flat pale landscape; warm ground even at dawn; enormous sky
- **China / Shanghai (June)**: Hot and humid; heavy air at night; Huangpu River busy by day, quieter at dawn
- **Japan / Hakata (June)**: Warm and somewhat humid; beginning of rainy season on main island
- **Japan / Hokkaido (June)**: Cooler than mainland; no full rainy season; clean pine-and-mineral air; evenings cold (around 4°C); stars very clear at night; rocky coast cold and wet at low tide
- **Antarctica (year-round)**: Extreme cold, blizzards, perpetual ice; daylight varies dramatically by season
