Third Return

The boy arrived in Yokohama on a Tuesday.

He left his bag in a room near the harbor and went to the pier. The cranes were in different positions from the last time. Four new ships at the berths. He took down the numbers.

In the evening, he opened his notebook to a clean page and tried to write a reason for coming here.

He could not find one.

He turned back through the pages. Every other arrival had a reason written before he left. Port: data changes daily. Southwest section incomplete. He found each one. Each time, he had written first, then moved.

For this arrival, there was nothing. He had woken up in Tokyo and packed. That was all.

He wrote one line on the clean page.

Arrived Yokohama. Reason: none recorded. First time.

He looked at the line for a while.

Then he closed the notebook and went to the window. The cranes were still. The harbor lights reflected off the water. He watched them until it was late.

The next morning he went back to the pier.
