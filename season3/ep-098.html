Season 3 · Ep 98

The Girl with Glasses

He was walking back along the harbour.

A girl passed him on the path — small, a school bag on her back, moving quickly. She was wearing glasses.

His hand moved.

He reached for his pencil before he knew he was doing it. He opened the notebook.

He looked at her back as she walked away. Her face had been fog, the way all faces were. He had not seen it. He could not record it.

He looked at what he had written.

Glasses.

He stood on the path with the notebook open.

He had recorded glasses before — as part of a physical description, as one measurement among others. But he had written this before he thought about it. His hand had moved on its own.

He looked at the word.

He looked at the path where she had gone.

He closed the notebook.

He stood there for a moment. Then he turned and walked toward the station.
