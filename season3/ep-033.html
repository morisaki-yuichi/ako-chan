Two Kinds of Unknown

0:00

One afternoon the otter floated and did not do anything the boy could name.

It was not eating. It was not diving. It was not watching the shore. It just floated, paws on its face, for a long time.

He waited. He had his notebook open. He tried one category, then another.

Not resting. Resting had a pattern: slower breathing, eyes mostly closed. This was different.

Not watching. Watching involved direction.

He tried four more. None of them fit.

He wrote: otter: floating. Activity: unclassified. Reason: unknown.

He looked at the word unknown. It was the only unknown in the whole Hokkaido record.

He thought about the Pattern A section. That also said unknown.

But they were different.

With the otter, he had all the data. He could see the otter fully. Every part of it was recordable. He just could not find the right category for what the otter was doing.

With Pattern A, the data did not form. He could not see the thing itself.

One unknown was inside a full record. The other was the missing record.

He wrote: not the same kind of unknown.

He closed the notebook. The otter kept floating.
