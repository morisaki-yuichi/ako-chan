Season 3 · Ep 70

Design

He designed for three days.

All the calculations were in his head. He had done them before, in smaller pieces: the camera module in the Mediterranean, the chip tests in Yokohama, the battery measurements on the ship.

Now he put them together.

The notebook was the output. He drew each part to scale. The optical system on page one. The processing layout on page two. The power circuit on page three. He left the housing open — he needed to know the total weight before he could shape the metal.

On the third afternoon he counted the components from his bag and weighed each one on the small scale.

The emperor penguin sat two metres away and watched.

The Adélie penguin walked around the equipment, stopping at each item. It said something about each one. The boy looked up once and went back to his drawing.

He finished the component list on the fourth morning. Ninety-four items accounted for. One remaining: the black stone from the ice.

Component 071.

He wrote: 95 components total.
