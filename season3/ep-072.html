Season 3 · Ep 72

Serial Numbers

On the last day of construction, the boy marked every component.

He had always done this. Every device he had ever made had numbered parts. He did not think about it when he started — he simply took his pencil and began at one end.

He marked the housing first. Then the internal frame. He opened the back panel and marked each chip, each connection point, each wire junction.

He wrote in very small, careful characters. He had practice.

When he reached the camera module, he wrote the number on the lens housing where it would be visible when the device was closed. When he reached component 071 — the black stone — he wrote its number on the smooth flat face pressed against the frame.

He counted as he went. Each number confirmed.

When he finished, he counted again from the list in his notebook.

Ninety-five components. All marked.

He closed the back panel. He held the device in his hand and looked at the side where the numbers were visible — a small column running down the housing.

He looked at them for a long time.
