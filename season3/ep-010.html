Season 3 · Ep 10

High Tide

On the fourth morning, the tide was high.

The boy was at the pier wall at 7:04. He took out his calipers and measured from the current waterline to the stain in the concrete: 1.09 metres. Close to his earlier reading of 1.2. He noted the difference and wrote both numbers.

He had three days of tide times now. The pattern was clear. High tide came between seven and eight each morning. Low tide came in the early afternoon. He could already predict the next high tide: tomorrow at approximately 7:50.

He walked to the far end of the basin. He had not measured the tide mark at Pier 3 yet. He went there now. He crouched at the wall and measured: 1.3 metres. Higher than the main pier. The wall faced a different angle. The waves reached further here.

He noted the difference and walked back.

He looked at all his Yokohama entries so far. Crane counts, berth numbers, tide marks. Every pattern was building. Each one would have more tomorrow.

Three cranes were moving near Berths 4 and 9. He had not counted today's moves yet.

He opened to a clean page. There was still a great deal to record.
