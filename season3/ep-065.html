Season 3 · Ep 65

The Missing Data

He looked at the words he had written.

Biased sample.

He needed the missing side. The species that were gone — removed from the record, many of them, by human decisions. Their behaviour, their patterns, their voices. He had none of this. He could not observe what was not here.

He turned to a clean page and wrote carefully.

Species extinguished by human activity: not recorded.

He looked at the line. He counted the categories: mammals, birds, reptiles, insects. Thousands of species, gone permanently. He had never been able to observe any of them. They were gone before he arrived.

He wrote two more lines.

Fundamental gap in dataset.

Judgment cannot be made.

He read the three lines back. His thesis about humanity could not be confirmed or denied with this data. The counter-evidence was biased. The main evidence was incomplete without the context of what had been lost.

He put his pencil down.

The wind moved across the ice.
