# Season 3 — World & Setting

Geography, locations, and key setting details. Update as stories establish new facts.

---

## Core Premise

S3 is set in **modern-day Japan and abroad** — several years after S2.
The boy travels alone, recording everything in notebooks. He has no home base,
only a sequence of observation sites.

Ako-chan now lives in **Yokohama** (drawn there by an unexplained pull after S2).
She is the centre of gravity the boy keeps returning to, without knowing it.

## Key Locations

### Tokyo — Room 204

- The boy's first base; second floor of a small apartment block
- **Room 204**: 3m 7cm wide × 4m 12cm long × 2m 30cm high; 4 power sockets; 2 windows; wall stain 8cm across (near corner)
- Station: 214 steps from Room 204
- Tokyo Station: 9 exits; 41 steps from platform to first gate
- The boy mapped the entire district in two weeks (grid method: north→south, east→west)
- **Shrine / park area**: bench 3 from the gate; sparrow wire; Suke-san's territory

### Yokohama — Pier 3

- The boy's repeated base throughout the year
- **Port structure**: cranes (14 counted on arrival), ships at berth, at anchor, in motion
- **Pier 3 end wall**: the boy's primary observation point; coordinates marked with a "?" on his map
- Tides follow a ~50-minute daily shift; verified against lunar cycle
- The port is the only place in the year where the boy writes "Return" in his travel log (four times total)

### Hokkaido — Rocky Shore

- Rocky coastline north of Hakodate; richer marine biology than Yokohama Harbour
- Sea temperature: 12°C (May); within Riro's preferred range
- Rock point: where the boy observes the sea otter for 20+ days (complete record)
- Same shore where the boy encounters Ako-chan, Dango, and Ruka for the fourth time

### Mediterranean — Rocky Ledge

- Coastal site with strong white light (higher colour temperature than Yokohama)
- Rocky shore, different from Hokkaido — more arid, exposed to sun
- Gabu and Daru's home territory: rocky ledge with day/shade split
- Night distance between Gabu and Daru: 0 cm (cold pushes them together)
- The boy counts 6 crabs, 3 snail types, 1 fish type, 4 anemone clusters in the rock pools

### Antarctica — Research Base

- Old, unmanned station; accessible; three rooms; workbench; heating; instruments
- Previous user's notebooks found on the shelf (daily temperature, pressure, wind; same column precision as the boy's method)
- Coordinates recorded in public databases; the boy does not know it is Yu-chan's future base
- Construction site for Obia; all serial numbers stamped here (No.001 onwards)
- **Pen-san** and **Colonel Adélie** become the boy's only unplanned community
- Scrap parts and fragments remain after Obia is complete

## S3 Narrative Rules (World Layer)

- Animal faces are **not fog** for the boy — he can record them fully
- Human faces are **always fog** — this is his permanent state, not a symptom
- The "hole" (Pattern A) is specific to Ako-chan: not face-fog (universal), but a record that stays thin across multiple encounters
- The word "return" appears four times in the boy's Yokohama travel log — each time he writes it without intending to
- The reason column is left blank three times (01-25 Yokohama, 02-22 Yokohama, 04-16 Tokyo) — each blank marks an unexplained pull
