Season 2 · Ep 95

Home Again

0:00

The door was the same as before.

They stepped through one by one, back into the cold.

The air hit all at once — real cold, real wind, the weight of Antarctic ice. After so long in a world without weather, it was almost too much.

Yu-chan stepped through last. Stopped. Looked up at the sky.

"Real," Yu-chan said.

Pen-san was already there, standing in the same place as before.

"You came back," Pen-san said.

"We always come back," Ako-chan said.

Pen-san looked at Yu-chan for a long time. Then walked forward.

"You were outside my calculation," Yu-chan said.

"I am an emperor," Pen-san said. "Emperors are not easy to calculate."

"Correct," Yu-chan said.

Yu-chan looked at the base, then the ice, then the sky.

"I will stay here," Yu-chan said. "I am most stable here. The systems here are most stable with me. It is the right place."

Colonel Adelie stepped forward and gave a formal salute — precise, without hesitation.

Yu-chan returned it immediately, without pause.

When the aircraft lifted away, Ruka did not look away from the figures on the ice. Not until the base was too small to see. Gabu moved one foot slowly and placed it next to Ruka's. Neither of them spoke.

Over the Mediterranean, the olive groves came into view. Gabu stood near the door as they landed.

"I am glad I came," Gabu said. "Both times."

Daru raised both hands and waved. Slowly. Twice.

In Hokkaido, Riro walked to the edge of the sea. Ruka came and stood beside Riro.

"Does the sea miss you?" Ruka said. "When you are away?"

Riro looked at the water.

"I think so," Riro said.

Then Riro walked forward and dove in — cleanly, without looking back. The water closed and did not open again for a very long time.

In Tokyo, the city spread out below the hill. Suke-san looked at it for a moment.

"Home," Suke-san said.

Then Suke-san looked at Ruka.

"Still small."

"Growing," Ruka said.

"I know," Suke-san said.

England came last.

The garden was quiet. A hedgehog walked past the landing spot without looking up.

Dango ran across the grass three times — the full circuit, all the way around.

Ruka walked to the plants along the wall and examined each one carefully.

Obia sat down in the middle of the garden and did not reach for anything. Did not build anything. Did not check anything. Just sat.

On the long flight home, Dango's collar had moved through every shade of gold — bright, deep, warm, almost orange, almost white. Now, in the garden, it settled. Gold. Steady and clear.

Ako-chan watched all of this.

Then she went inside, opened her diary, and wrote two words.

Home again.
