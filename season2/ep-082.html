Season 2 · Ep 82

The Core

The corridor ended and they saw it.

The Core was enormous. It was a sphere made of layered light. Dim, shifting, alive with movement. It rose from the floor and disappeared into the ceiling far above.

The surface was not smooth. Cracks ran across it from top to bottom. Some were thin, like lines in old glass. Others were wide enough to see through.

Inside the cracks, loops of light moved constantly. They started. They broke apart. They started again.

"This is the original system," said Obia. "Everything in this world runs from here. When the Core fragments, everything fragments."

Ako-chan stepped forward and closed her eyes.

The signals hit her all at once. Hundreds of voices, all coming at the same moment, all repeating their own loop.

One said: "the consensus was — the consensus was —"

Another: "if the system fails — if the system fails —"

They were not speaking to her. They were playing back. Stuck.

But Ako-chan did not pull away. She listened. In the spaces between the loops, she found the shape of what had happened.

Fear. Argument. Decision. Something that broke.

She opened her eyes.

"The Core is replaying itself," she said. "It has been doing this for a very long time."

"That is why the system is degrading," said Obia. "It cannot move forward. Only back."

Dango sat down. His collar glowed soft gold. He did not look afraid. He looked steady.
