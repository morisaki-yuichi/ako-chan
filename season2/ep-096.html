Season 2 · Ep 96

Riro's Answer

0:00

A robot came to Riro.

"You spoke earlier about a sea urchin," it said. "On a difficult day. You said it was the best thing in the world."

"Yes," said Riro.

"Which urchin, in your life, was the best one you have ever eaten?"

Riro did not answer immediately.

He stood for a moment with his paws at his sides, his eyes looking at nothing in particular.

"There was a morning," he said slowly. "A long time ago. The current had been strong for three days. I had not found food. Then the current shifted — just for a short while — and I found one urchin on one particular rock."

"Was it the largest urchin?"

"No."

"The most well-formed?"

"No. It was ordinary. A perfectly ordinary urchin."

"Then why was it the best?"

"Because of the three days before it," Riro said. "Because of how hard the current had been. Because I had not expected to find it." He looked at the robot. "If I could eat that urchin whenever I wished, it would not be the best. It would become ordinary. The best things happen once. That is why they are the best."

The robot was quiet.

"We will need time to understand this," it said.

"Yes," said Riro. "Most things worth understanding take time."
