Talking About the Journey

Two hours north of the Antarctic, Daru opened his notebook.

"I think we should talk about it," he said.

Everyone looked at him.

"All of it. The whole journey. In order. I have been keeping notes. I can go through them. But I want everyone to add what I missed."

Suke-san made a sound that meant yes.

Daru began.

He started with the entry point—the tunnel under the base, the first gate, the silver city. He described Unit Eleven standing still for fifty years. He described Suke-san's objection in the Governance Center.

When he mentioned the off-note in the Music Hall, Riro nodded once.

When he got to the moment Ako-chan first heard Yu-chan's signal inside the Core, he paused.

"I did not see that part," he said. "I was too far away."

Ako-chan described it.

Daru wrote without stopping.

They flew north for many hours. The sky turned from white to blue to deep gold.

By the time Daru finished, everyone had spoken.

The journey was now a shared thing.
