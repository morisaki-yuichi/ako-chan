Season 2 · Ep 38

Obia's Limit

Obia walked forward and stood in front of the robot.

It sent a standard greeting. Clear and precise. The kind any networked system should recognize.

The robot received it. Then it responded — in a format Obia had never encountered before. Long. Dense. Structured in a way it could not immediately follow.

It tried again. Simpler. Then again. It decoded parts. Useful fragments. But not all.

It stood very still. This was new.

Later, while the others rested, Obia came to Ako-chan and spoke quietly.

"I need to tell you something."

"Tell me."

"The system here is more advanced than I expected. The way they exchange information — it is beyond what I can fully decode." It paused. "In several important areas."

Ako-chan looked at it.

"So you cannot talk to them properly?"

"I can talk to them. But I cannot follow everything they say. They can follow everything I say without difficulty." Its eyes were still. "I am at a disadvantage."

"Thank you for telling me," said Ako-chan. "But you found Yu-chan's signal. You built the frame analysis. Being limited is not the same as being unable to help."

Obia said nothing more.

But something was lighter than it had been before.
