Fixed Sun

0:00

There was light in the garden. Good light — not too bright, not too dark. The kind of light plants need.

Daru looked up.

"Where is the sun?" Daru said.

Ako-chan looked up too. There was no sun — just a steady, even brightness spread across the whole sky.

"The light source is fixed," the guide said. "We calculated the optimal level for growth. It does not change."

Daru looked at the plants. Then at the sky. Then at the plants again.

"In Greece," Daru said, "the sun moves. Every hour, it is in a different place. The plants know this. They know where the sun will be next. So they reach — just a little — toward where it will go."

Gabu had come to stand beside Daru.

"They reach because something will change," Gabu said. "If nothing changes, there is nothing to reach for."

The guide was quiet for a moment.

"We removed the variation," it said. "Variation seemed inefficient."

Gabu looked at a tall plant. The plant was perfectly still. Its leaves were pointing in no particular direction — not up, not sideways. Just there.

"Yes," Gabu said. "You did."
