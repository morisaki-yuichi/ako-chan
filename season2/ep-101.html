The Music and the Door

The path back passed the Music Hall.

Riro slowed.

The doors were open. Music came out—not like the perfect pieces from before. Rougher. There were moments where two instruments seemed uncertain. But it moved. Something new grew out of what had come before.

Riro stopped and listened for a full minute.

Then he said, very quietly: "Not bad."

The sound drifted through the open doors. Inside, the orchestra kept playing.

The group walked on—nine of them now. Yu-chan walked in the middle, between Obia and Gabu.

After a while, Gabu glanced across at his.

"I wanted to meet you properly," Gabu said.

"I know," said Yu-chan. "I watched you for a long time."

"That is a little strange."

"Yes," said Yu-chan. "I know."

"Was I what you expected?" Gabu asked.

Yu-chan considered. "No. You were quieter. And more important." She paused. "I did not expect both."

Gabu was silent. Then his tail moved—just once, slowly.

They walked the rest of the way in comfortable silence.

The door was ahead.
