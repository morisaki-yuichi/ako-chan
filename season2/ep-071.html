Season 2 · Ep 71

The First Split Vote

Obia had spent six days with the council managers.

It showed them data. Every objection Suke-san had raised had led to a better proposal. The improvement was measurable.

"Disagreement improves outcomes," it said. "This is your own data."

The managers approved a new protocol. Every proposal, before going to a vote, would require a counter-argument. The counter-argument did not have to be a final position. It only had to be a different view.

Three days after the protocol began, the first split vote came.

A proposal about resource allocation. A council robot had been studying Suke-san's methods. It raised a question about Zone Seven's long-term projections.

Some robots found the counter-argument persuasive. Others did not.

The vote happened.

One hundred and forty-two in favor. Thirty-one opposed.

The hall went completely still.

The result hung above the platform: 142 to 31.

It was the first non-unanimous vote in four years, two months, and eleven days.

Then a low hum moved through the hall. Not loud. Not structured. Not speech.

"What is that?" Ako-chan asked.

Obia listened. "I believe it is the sound of one hundred and seventy-three robots processing a result their system has not seen before," it said. "And finding it — not wrong."

Dango's collar went bright gold. Not the quiet kind. The wide-awake kind.
