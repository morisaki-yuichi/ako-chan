Season 2 · Ep 107

Two Farewells

They landed near Suke-san's hill. The air was cold and dry.

Riro would stay a few days. He did not say why, and no one asked.

Riro came to the edge of the landing area before Suke-san did.

He stood in front of Dango. He looked at him the way he looked at food worth considering — careful, exact.

"You have changed," he said.

Dango's tail moved once. "Yes."

"That is correct," said Riro.

He came to Ruka next. He looked at Ruka for a moment with the same careful attention as their first meeting. "Still interesting," he said.

Ruka stood straight. "Thank you."

He came last to Ako-chan. He looked at her for a moment.

"The journey was good," he said. "Both times. Not bad."

Everyone who had traveled with Riro understood what that meant.

Suke-san walked to the edge of the landing area and turned back to the group. His eyes found Ruka.

"Still small," he said.

Ruka stood straight. "I am growing."

"I know," said Suke-san.

The words were the same as the first time. But the weight was not—and everyone who had been there for the first time knew the difference.

Suke-san touched Dango's nose with his own, once. Then he looked at Ako-chan.

"Good journey," he said.

"Good journey," said Ako-chan.

He turned toward the hill. He did not look back.

The airship flew south.

The olive grove was green even in December. They landed at its edge, and immediately the smell was different—earth and leaves and something old and warm.

Gabu stepped off and pressed his front paws into the soil. He stayed like that for a moment, eyes closed.

Daru turned to Ruka. "We will meet again," he said. "I have decided."

"When?" said Ruka.

"I do not know yet. But I have decided it will happen, so it will."

Ruka looked at him for a moment, then nodded once, seriously.

Then Gabu came to Ako-chan.

"Good that you came again," he said. "Both times."

The two words—both times—held the whole first journey in them. And this one. And the years between.

"Thank you," said Ako-chan. "For being here. Both times."

Gabu pressed his head once, gently, against Ako-chan's shoulder. Then he stepped back.
