Season 2 · Ep 73

The Music Hall

The group came through a high arched entrance and stopped.

The hall was enormous. At its centre, a full orchestra of robots performed. Every arm and bow and finger moved in exact time. The music filled the entire space evenly — no corner too loud, no corner too quiet. Each note was where it should be.

Dango sat down immediately. Ruka made one small circle and lay down with its ears up.

"How long have they been performing?" Ako-chan asked.

"Always," Obia said. "The same eight hundred and forty-seven pieces. In the same order. Each performance identical to the last."

Riro did not move from where he stood near the entrance. He listened through one piece, then another, then a third. The light did not change. No one disturbed him.

When the fourth piece ended, Riro turned.

"Technically," he said, "this is the best music I have heard in my life."

A pause.

"The balance is exact. The phrasing is optimal. No weak section. No moment where I would change anything."

A longer pause.

"And it is the least interesting music I have ever heard."

Nobody spoke.

Riro looked back at the orchestra. Already two minutes into the fifth piece. Precise. Flawless.

"Something is missing," he said. "I cannot name it yet. But I know what it feels like when food is technically correct and has no life in it. This is the same feeling."

Gabu said, from his corner near the far wall: "Take your time."

"I know," Riro said. "I am."
