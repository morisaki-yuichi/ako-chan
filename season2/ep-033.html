Who Guards the Door

They talked about it together. All ten of them sat in the main room. The question was simple: who would go through the frame?

Before the conversation had gone far, Pen-san moved.

He took one step back. Then he was still.

Everyone looked at him.

"My place is here," said Pen-san. His voice was steady. "Someone must guard this door from the outside. You need a way to come back. You need someone on this side who knows the frame and knows the plan." He looked at Ako-chan. "I will guard it. Until you return."

Colonel Adelie stepped forward. "I agree. Two at this position is correct."

It was settled. Eight would go in. Two would hold the door.

That afternoon, Obia explained Pen-san's three tasks: keep the frame powered, maintain the communication link, send the recall signal if anything changed. Pen-san listened to all of it without asking questions. Obia gave him a small flat device. Pen-san pressed it against his chest with his flipper.

Ako-chan found Colonel Adelie outside, looking at the ice.

"External monitoring. Communication support. I will check the perimeter daily," said Colonel Adelie. He looked at the horizon. "The base will hold."

That was all he needed to say.
