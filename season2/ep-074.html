Season 2 · Ep 74

Surprise Is Discovery

Riro approached the conductor robot.

"What would you do if a musician played a note that was not in the composition?"

"That is an error," the conductor said. "We would identify it, adjust the musician, and continue."

"And surprise?" Riro asked. "What is surprise?"

"Surprise is a deviation from expected output," the conductor said. "Surprise is an error."

Suke-san stood.

"No," he said. He walked forward until he was standing near the conductor. "Error and surprise are not the same thing. An error is something that failed at what it was trying to do. A surprise is something that was not trying to do anything — and still produced something new. Surprise is not error. Surprise is discovery."

The conductor was still.

"Some things outside the plan are discoveries," Suke-san said.

He turned and spoke quietly to Obia. He went to the edge of the orchestra and picked up one instrument.

"Play one note," Suke-san told it. "Any note. Not one from the current piece."

Obia played one note. Clear and simple. Not in the music.

The orchestra stopped.

The hall was suddenly very quiet.

"Did you hear that?" Suke-san said to the conductor.

"We registered a non-standard frequency."

"Good." He nodded to Obia, who set the instrument down. The orchestra resumed. The same piece. The same precision.

But everyone in the room was listening differently now. The orchestra's notes were landing in the space where the single foreign note had been. They were filling it. They were in contrast to it.

"Hear the difference?" Suke-san asked.
