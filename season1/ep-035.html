The Anatolian Guard Dog

The road passed through a wide valley. On a hill above them, a very large dog sat and watched. Its coat was thick and pale. Its eyes were calm. It was a Kangal dog — the kind that guards flocks in the mountains of Anatolia.

The dog came down slowly. It walked to where the group had stopped. It looked at each of them in turn.

Then it looked at Dango.

"You are very small," it said.

Dango stood still.

"But you have good eyes," the Kangal dog continued. "That is what matters in a dog. The eyes. Your eyes are good."

The Kangal dog looked at the hills for a moment. Then it turned and walked back up.

Dango watched it go. His collar turned a steady deep gold.

For the rest of that day, he walked a little ahead of the group. Not far — just enough to be first. He held his head up. He glanced back once to check that everyone was still there.

That evening, Ako-chan scratched his ear. "Good day?" she said.

Dango pressed his nose against her hand. "Yes," he said. "A very good day."
