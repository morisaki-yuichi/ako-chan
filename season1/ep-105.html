Obia's Confession, Part One

Obia began to speak.

"I was made by a scientist," it said. "I was not born. I was built. I was given one purpose: to travel with you, to watch you, and to record what I observed."

Nobody moved.

"The reason I appeared in January was not chance. I was placed near your home on the first day of the year. Everything that followed was planned."

Ako-chan's face changed. She had started to say something — and then she stopped. She was not afraid. She was not shouting. She was thinking. Taking it in. Turning it over very slowly inside.

Obia continued. "The anomalies — the whale in the harbour, the frozen sea, the dry riverbed — none of them were accidents. Someone made them happen. Someone designed each one. And I was sent to watch how you responded."

"The structure here, under the ice, is the centre of it all. This is where the signals came from. This is where the records were sent."

Dango made a low sound in his throat. His collar was deep orange. He did not move, but his eyes did not leave Obia's face.

Obia looked back at him.

"I have more to say," said Obia. "But that is the first part."

It waited.
