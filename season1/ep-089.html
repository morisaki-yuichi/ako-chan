The White Horizon

At dawn on the last day of October, Obia looked south.

There was something on the horizon.

It was thin — just a line, barely there in the pale morning light. But it was there, and it was white. Whiter than clouds. Whiter than the spray. A different kind of white.

"Ako-chan," Obia said.

She was already awake. She came to the railing.

She looked where Obia was looking.

"Is that—" she said.

"That is Antarctica," Obia said.

The others woke up one by one. Daru scrambled out of his unit. Gabu came slowly but he came. Riro surfaced and pulled himself up. Suke-san appeared at the railing. Ako-chan held Dango.

Dango looked at the horizon for a moment. Then he walked to the very front of the deck and stood there, ears straight up into the wind. He did not make a sound. He just faced south.

All seven of them stood in silence and looked at the white line.

Ten months and thirty-one days since England. One white line on the horizon, growing slowly brighter.

They were almost there.
