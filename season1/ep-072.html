Suke-san in Santiago

0:00

Santiago was stone and glass and wide roads.

Suke-san walked without speaking. He looked at the buildings. At his own feet on the pavement.

"There are no forests," he said. "No roots. No moss. No smell of rain in the ground."

Dango said nothing for a moment. Then: "I felt the same in Tokyo once. I wanted to leave after one day."

Suke-san was quiet.

After two days they left the city.

The road went south into the lake district. Trees stood tall and close together. The ground was dark. The air smelled of rain and roots and things growing.

Suke-san stopped walking.

Then he started running.

He ran through the trees for a long time. His tail went straight. He was not being careful. He just ran, full speed.

Dango ran beside him.

When they stopped, both of them stood breathing hard. Suke-san looked at the trees.

"This is correct," he said.

"Yes," said Dango.

Suke-san looked at him. "When this is over — you should come to the Hokkaido hill. You said you would come."

"You said it was allowed," said Dango.

"It is," said Suke-san.

He said it simply. His tail was fully curled. He meant it.
