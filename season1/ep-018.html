A Travel Diary

In a small shop near the market, Ako-chan found a notebook. It was dark blue. The cover was smooth. The pages inside were blank and very white.

She bought it.

That evening, sitting on the bed in the guesthouse, she opened it to the first page.

She wanted to write about the journey. She wanted to write about the anomalies and the animals and the cities. But she found herself thinking about Obia.

She wrote: I am glad it is here.

Then she stopped.

What was Obia? A guide? A traveler? A protector? A mystery?

She tried again. Obia is like—

No. That was not right either.

Dango was asleep near her feet. Obia was sitting by the window, its silver shape quiet against the dark glass.

Ako-chan looked at it for a long time.

She wrote: I do not know how to describe Obia yet. But I know this: I am glad it is here.

She read it back. It was not a perfect sentence. But it felt true.

She closed the notebook. Obia did not turn from the window.

Outside, the city was quiet. The Danube moved past in the dark below.
