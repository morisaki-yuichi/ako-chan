The Dog on the Hill

The hill looked out over the sea. The grass was short and green and the wind moved through it in slow waves.

At the top of the hill, a small dog sat perfectly still. He faced the sea. His back was to them as they came up the path.

He did not turn.

They came closer. The dog was a Shiba Inu. Small and neat. His tail was curled tight. His posture was very correct.

They stopped a few steps behind him.

The dog spoke. He did not turn his head.

"I knew you were coming," he said.

Ako-chan looked at Obia. Obia looked at the dog.

"How?" said Ako-chan.

"The birds," said the dog. "Also the fox. Also the wind, a little." A pause. "I have been watching this coast for a long time. I know when something new arrives."

He turned then. He was smaller than Dango. His face was calm and precise. His eyes went around the group.

When his eyes reached Obia, one ear moved. Just a little. It tilted back. Then it went straight again.

The dog's expression did not change.

"My name is Suke-san," he said.

"I am Ako-chan," she said. She introduced everyone in order.

Suke-san nodded once for each name. He looked at the sea again.

"You are going further east," he said. It was not a question.

"Yes," said Ako-chan.

Suke-san said nothing for a moment.

"Good hill," said Daru cheerfully, from on top of the backpack.

"Yes," said Suke-san. "It is."

The wind came off the sea and pushed through the grass around them.
