The Night Signal

At two in the morning, the room was very quiet.

Ako-chan and Dango were both asleep. Dango made a small sound in his dream.

Obia was awake. It was always awake.

Something was arriving. A signal. Very faint. From far to the south. It came in a pattern: short, pause, long, pause, short, short.

Obia listened for a long time.

The signal was not from any station it knew. It was not from a ship. It was not a normal frequency. But it was regular. Very regular. Something was making it on purpose.

Obia opened the record device slowly, so the screen would not wake Ako-chan.

Then it stopped.

It did not add the signal to the shared record. It saved it somewhere else instead. Somewhere only it could read.

Obia sat still in the dark room for a long time. It listened to the signal come and come and come from the south. It did not feel like nothing. It felt like something it could not yet name.

Dango made another small sound. His collar glowed orange for just a moment, then faded back to gold.

Obia watched him sleep. It did not tell Ako-chan about the signal in the morning.
