The Emperor's Eyes

One morning, Pen-san crossed the camp and sat beside Ako-chan.

It had not done this before. It just came and sat. Very close. Its round body pressed gently against her side.

Ako-chan went still.

Pen-san looked straight ahead. It did not explain itself.

Then it looked up at her.

Its eyes were dark. Very round. Very still. The largest eyes Ako-chan had ever seen. She had noticed them before — everyone had, the first day. But this was different. This was close. This was looking back at her.

She tried to look away. She could not.

She tried to think about breakfast. She could not think about breakfast.

She opened her mouth.

"...Can I hold you?" she said.

Then she stopped. She had not decided to say that. The words had come out before she chose them.

Pen-san blinked.

"If you like," it said.

Ako-chan picked up Pen-san carefully. Pen-san was warm and round and its down was impossibly soft.

From behind her, she heard a very small sound. Like a short breath.

She looked back.

Dango's mouth was a little open. His collar had turned orange.

"Ah," he said. Very quietly.

Pen-san turned its head to look at Dango. It looked very calm. Very comfortable. Very settled.

Dango looked at Pen-san. He looked at Ako-chan. He looked at Pen-san again.
